How to read a Flow Tracker card

Each card is one ticker over your chosen horizon (5d or 15d). The header badges tell you the shape of the setup at a glance; the metrics table below expands on the numbers; the "Why" line calls out the two or three components doing the most work.

Three badges to anchor on:

  1. Conviction (e.g. 7.4/10 A-) — how good the options flow is on its own. Pure flow quality, no price confirmation baked in.
  2. Stack (e.g. Stack 49 D) — the same flow plus everything else (dark pool, chains, insider, price, dealer). Tells you "how many independent signals agree."
  3. Direction + trend — NET BULLISH / BEARISH and ACCELERATING / STEADY / FADING. If these disagree with the scores, the scores usually win.

Tip: if the 0-10 conviction and the Stack score disagree a lot, the flow is strong but not confirmed by price, DP, insiders, or chain structure. That's a flow-only setup — size smaller.

Scoring rules

Four distinct numbers, each measuring a different thing. They share some raw inputs but live at different levels of abstraction.

Conviction score (0-10) + letter grade

A weighted blend of the six flow-quality components, scaled 0-10 and mapped to a letter grade:

  • Persistence — how many of the window's days had directional flow.
  • Intensity — premium / market cap (how big relative to the name).
  • Consistency — daily premium stability vs a single-day spike.
  • Acceleration — is the premium trend rising? (t-stat on the daily slope).
  • Mass — absolute cumulative premium.
  • OI change — directional open-interest move, filtered for sane ranges.

The blend is then multiplied by the DTE bucket (0-7d softens 10%, 31-90d and 91+d boost 5%) and haircut by 15% if the window put/call ratio flags a hedging risk. A small dark-pool alignment bonus is applied later in the server (up to ~10%) when DP bias agrees with flow direction.

Window return does NOT adjust this score — it lives only in the Stack. Keeps "conviction" a pure flow-quality read that doesn't degrade just because price hasn't confirmed yet.

Conviction Stack (0-100) + letter tier

Multi-signal confluence. Six capped components, summing to 100:

ComponentMaxWhat it measures
Flow core50Conviction score × 5. This is why Stack moves with the 0-10.
Dark pool15DP bias and notional-per-mcap; + z-bonus when DP volume is historically hot.
Price12Window return + session tone + relative strength. This is where the +X% pill contributes.
Chain10Strike structure — single-strike focus vs smeared flow.
Insider8Recent insider buys/sells aligned with the trade direction.
Dealer5Dealer hedging bias (chase / supply / neutral).

Tier labels:

  • Stack A (Elite, 80+) — everything agrees. Rare.
  • Stack B (Strong, 65-79) — flow plus at least 2-3 other signals.
  • Stack C (Moderate, 50-64) — flow plus one confirmation.
  • Stack D (Weak, <50) — flow only, no confluence.

A high 0-10 with a Stack D means the options flow is strong but nothing else is confirming yet. Still tradable, but smaller size / tighter stop.

Accumulation score (0-100)

Measures shape of the pattern rather than magnitude. Fixed weights:

  • 0.35 × days of active flow in the window
  • 0.30 × one-sidedness (share of premium on the dominant side)
  • 0.25 × rising trend (acceleration t-stat)
  • 0.10 × premium / market cap in bps

Used as the gate for the Accumulation mode toggle and as one component of the "Why" line on the card. The ACC badge is no longer shown on the card header to reduce clutter — the score itself is still computed and still drives the mode filter.

Window return (+/- X%)

Price change of the underlying over the tracker window (5 or 15 trading days). Shown as a colored pill — green when aligned with the trade direction, orange when fighting.

Feeds the Stack's price component (up to 12 pts). Does not move the 0-10 conviction score (by design — conviction is flow-only).

"Fighting flow" with a large magnitude (e.g. bullish flow while price is down 5%) is a specific signature: buyers haven't been paid yet. Often late-chase risk; sometimes a setup before a reversal. Treat as a question, not an answer.

Z-tier (z30 / z·shrunk / z·peer / abs)

Confidence label for the rolling z-score baseline. Z-scoring measures how unusual today's flow is vs the ticker's own recent history; when history is thin we fall back step by step:

  1. z30 (Tier 1) — ≥20 days of own history. Full per-ticker z-score. Most trustworthy.
  2. z·shrunk (Tier 2) — 5-19 days of own history. Shrunken toward the cross-section to avoid over-fitting to a short sample.
  3. z·peer (Tier 3) — <5 days of own history. Uses today's cross-sectional cohort as the baseline. Still useful, but less ticker-specific.
  4. abs (Tier 4) — absolute hand-tuned thresholds. No history available at all.

The z-tier is a caveat, not a magnitude. A "z·peer A-" setup isn't weaker than a "z30 A-" — we just have less data behind it.

Why line (grade reasons)

The "Why" row on the card decomposes the conviction score into its top two or three drivers, plus context bullets that don't directly move the grade but matter for interpretation.

  • Driver — component that pushed the score up.
  • Drag — component that pulled the score down.
  • Context-driver / context-drag — informational (e.g. "price confirms flow", "weekly DTE"). These are 0-point bullets, they're there for color.

Mode toggle (Strong / Accum / All)

Three progressively stricter filters on the Flow Tracker list:

  • All — every ticker that cleared the base qualifying flow gates.
  • Accumulation — subset: accumulation score above threshold AND persistence across the window AND sane liquidity.
  • Strong — subset of Accumulation: grade at least A-, plus additional gates on intensity and OI.

The three are strict subsets: Strong ⊆ Accumulation ⊆ All. Header counts like "3 strong / 8 accum / 14 all" always count the full population before the hard-filter cut.

Horizons (5d / 15d)

Toggle between two lookback windows:

  • 5d — aggressive. Surfaces acceleration and fresh positioning. Use when looking for entries on names that have started moving recently.
  • 15d — patient. Surfaces sustained accumulation. Use to find names where flow has been relentless for three weeks without a headline.

The "2H" agreement chip on each card tells you whether both horizons surface this ticker (with matching direction). "2H ↑ 100%" means both windows agree and the flow is bullish across both.

Warmup banner

When you see "Rebuilding history (day X of 3)" at the top, the multi-day flow regression is still warming up. Flow Tracker results are visible but should be treated with caution until the banner clears — the day-over-day comparisons are only as reliable as the history they draw from.

Typical cause: a snapshot purge, a fresh install, or a config change that invalidated the previous regression window.

Tabs & workflow

The sidebar groups tabs by what you do with them:

Decide

  • Overview — the shortlist: "trades to take now" and promotion candidates.
  • Candidates — full set of names that passed the scoring pipeline but aren't yet on the watchlist.
  • Watchlist — names you've explicitly flagged to monitor.

Execute

  • Positions — open trades with live structure, stops, and sizing.
  • Risk — portfolio-level regime, sizing multiplier, HALT caveats.

Context

  • Flow Tracker — multi-day regression of unusual options flow. The main discovery surface.
  • Unusual Flow — raw single-day unusual prints.
  • Dark Pool — block prints with side inference.
  • Hot Chains — most-active strikes across the universe.
  • Near Misses — names that almost qualified; useful for spotting early setups.

Review

  • Trade Log — every trade closed, with outcome.
  • Performance — PnL attribution.
  • Agent Shadow — what the agent would have done.
  • Search — free-form ticker lookup across history.

Chip glossary

Everything else you might see on a card, in alphabetical-ish order:

Direction & trend

  • NET BULLISH / NET BEARISH — which side carried the majority of unusual premium in the window.
  • ACCELERATING / STEADY / FADING — slope of daily premium. Accelerating = today's premium bigger than the window average.
  • 2H ↑ N% — both horizons (5d AND 15d) surface this ticker with aligned direction; N% is the agreement confidence.

Liquidity & structure

  • DEEP / HEALTHY / THIN / ILLIQUID — ADV-based liquidity tier. ILLIQUID rows are hard-filtered from Flow Tracker since you can't really fill there.
  • Debit spread / Long call / Long put / ATM straddle — suggested trade structure given IV rank, premium budget, and direction.

External signals

  • DP tier / DP aligned — dark pool z-tier and whether its bias agrees with flow direction. Drives the Stack's DP component and a small bonus on the 0-10.
  • Sentiment pill — session tone (STRENGTH / WEAKNESS / NEUTRAL) derived from intraday price/volume signature.
  • ER / Earnings badge — days to next reported earnings. Red when within the window; scale position sizing accordingly.
  • Sector chip — sector tag; when multiple cards in the same sector + direction show up, the card also shows a "+N in sector" cross-reference.

Metrics table

  • Prem / mcap bps — unusual premium as basis points of market cap. 50+ bps is large.
  • Cumulative premium — raw dollar premium over the window; total with bullish share in parens.
  • Active days — days in the window with qualifying flow.
  • Avg vol vs 30d — today's options volume in multiples of the 30-day average.
  • OI change — net open-interest change on the dominant side.
  • IV rank — 0-100 percentile of current implied vol against the past year.
  • P/C ratio — window put/call ratio; relative (vs ticker baseline) if history allows, absolute otherwise.
  • Avg |Δ| — premium-weighted average option delta. Higher = more directional conviction; "PRX" tag = delta came from proxy greeks rather than UW quotes.
  • t= — acceleration t-stat. Above +1.5 is meaningful rising; below -1.5 is meaningful fading.

Unusual Flow — Trader Card

The Unusual Flow tab is a scan-only recap: "what has the hottest unusual flow right now?" Every number on these cards comes from the current pipeline scan (flow_features_*.csv + today's normalized alert feed). For multi-day persistence, accumulation, and window return, use the Flow Tracker tab instead — a bridge link is shown on the modal when a ticker has history there.

Card header chips

  • LONG / SHORT — dominant side today, chosen by whichever of bullish_flow_intensity or bearish_flow_intensity is higher.
  • Grade (e.g. B+ 6.6) — conviction grade and 0-10 flow score for the dominant side. Same scale as the Flow Tracker conviction chip but computed on this scan only.
  • DTE bucket (LOTTERY / SWING / LEAP / OTHER) — which Days-To-Expiry bucket holds the largest share of today's unusual premium for the dominant side. Definitions:
    • LOTTERY — 0-14 DTE. Event speculation, earnings bets, squeezes. Fast decay; binary exposure.
    • SWING — 30-120 DTE. Institutional swing window; the signal-pipeline sweet spot.
    • LEAP — 180+ DTE. Deep positional / long-dated commitment.
    • OTHER — 15-29 or 121-179 DTE, the gaps between the canonical buckets.
  • Stack (e.g. Stack 49 D) — same 0-100 composite as Flow Tracker. Top flow cards carry a lighter input set (no DP / hot-chain), so stack here leans on flow-score + dealer regime.
  • Dealer chip (CHASE / PIN) — market-maker hedge bias from signal enrichment. CHASE = dealers forced to buy as price rises; PIN = pin-risk near a large strike.
  • ER Nd — earnings within 2 weeks; red tone if ≤ 5 days. Flags binary risk around a print.
  • Size label (Outsized / Notable / Normal / Minor) — flow intensity as basis points of market cap. Outsized > 5 bps, Notable 1-5 bps, Normal 0.1-1 bps, Minor < 0.1 bps.

Quality-signal chips (below the metrics)

  • Sweep N% — what percent of qualifying prints on the dominant side were sweeps (urgency orders that cleared multiple exchanges at once). Higher means more aggressive positioning.
  • Multileg N% — what percent of qualifying prints were part of multi-leg structures (spreads, combos). Higher means more defined-risk / hedged flow and less outright directional conviction.
  • Repeat Nx — today's repeat-flow acceleration ratio on the dominant side. 1.0 is flat vs. the preceding baseline; the chip warms at ≥ 1.3x and flashes hot at ≥ 2.0x — a sign of fresh conviction vs. rehashed positioning.

Premium-mix mini-bar

The thin stacked bar at the bottom of each card shows lottery / swing / leap dollar share of today's total unusual premium (both sides combined). Hover any segment for the exact dollar value. A bar dominated by swing tells you most of the money is in the institutional window; a bar dominated by lottery tells you it's short-dated speculation.

Trader Card modal (click any card)

Tapping a card opens a modal with six tabs:

  • Summary — every metric above in one view, plus dealer hedge, catalyst, and (when present) a bridge link to the Flow Tracker tab for the same ticker.
  • Premium Mix — the full DTE-bucket breakdown (bullish / bearish / percent-of-total for lottery, swing, leap, and other). Same payload the Flow Tracker modal uses.
  • Why? — plain-English narrative bullets explaining what's driving the flow score and direction.
  • Structure — recommended option structure (primary vehicle + alternatives + avoid + caveats) for this setup.
  • Trades — top 10 contributing prints (strike / expiry / DTE / bucket / premium / side) so you can see the actual LEAPS / swing / lottery contracts that drive the aggregated numbers.
  • Chart — full TradingView chart for the ticker with the same EMA studies used elsewhere.

Tip: if a card has a high grade but the bucket is LOTTERY and the Repeat chip is flat, you're looking at fresh but ephemeral speculation. A high grade with bucket SWING + repeat ≥ 1.3x is much more likely to be real institutional money.
